The main facts and figures show that:

  • in 2015/16, pupils from the Traveller of Irish Heritage and Gypsy/Roma ethnic groups had the highest rates of both fixed period and permanent exclusions

  • Asian and Chinese pupils had the lowest fixed period exclusion rates

  • Black Caribbean pupils were permanently excluded at 3 times the rate of White British pupils

  • across the broad ethnic groups, Black and Mixed ethnicity pupils had the highest rates of both fixed period and permanent exclusions

Things you need to know

Some of the statistics quoted here are based on very small numbers of pupils, and are highly variable over time. For example, only 111 Traveller of Irish Heritage pupils went to a special school in January 2016.

The Department for Education (DfE) has excluded, or ‘suppressed’, very small numbers (for example, values of 1 or 2, a percentage based on 1 or 2 pupils who achieved, or 0, 1 or 2 pupils who did not achieve a particular level).

This is because, where the size of the ethnic group population is small enough that an individual’s identity could be revealed, information is suppressed to preserve confidentiality. This is consistent with DfE’s statistical policy statement on confidentiality (PDF).

What the data measures

This data measures the rate of temporary ('fixed period') and permanent exclusions in schools in England. The figures are broken down by ethnicity and type of school (primary, secondary and special).

Data is presented separately for fixed period (temporary) exclusions and permanent exclusions:

  • a permanent exclusion refers to a pupil who is excluded and who will not return to the school they're excluded from (unless the exclusion is overturned)
  • a fixed period exclusion refers to a pupil who is excluded from a school for a set period of time (from part of the school day to 45 school days)

Pupils can be excluded multiple times, as long as all fixed periods of exclusion for a pupil in an academic year don’t add up to more than 45 school days (including exclusions from previous schools).

As pupils can receive more than one fixed period exclusion, in some cases the exclusion rate may be above 100%.

This data records the number of occasions on which a pupil was excluded, not the number of pupils that were excluded, nor how often they were excluded. Individuals may therefore be included in the data more than once. This means that the exclusion rates should be considered with some caution.

Exclusions data relates to all exclusions reported across the full academic year. Exclusion rates are calculated as a percentage of the number of pupils registered as at the January school census.

The ethnic categories used in this data

This data uses categories from the Department for Education’s school census, which is broadly based on the 2001 national Census, with 2 exceptions:

  • Traveller of Irish Heritage and Gypsy/Roma children have been separated into 2 categories
  • Chinese pupils have been assigned a separate category

These changes were made after consultations with local authorities and lobby groups.

The categories in the school census are as follows:

White:

  • White British
  • White Irish
  • Traveller of Irish Heritage
  • Gypsy/Roma
  • Other White

Mixed/Multiple ethnic groups:

  • White and Black Caribbean
  • White and Black African
  • White and Asian
  • Other Mixed background

Asian/Asian British:

  • Indian
  • Pakistani
  • Bangladeshi
  • Sri Lankan
  • Other Asian background

Black/African/Caribbean/Black British:

  • Black African
  • Black Caribbean
  • Other Black background

Chinese Other ethnic group

Information is provided for both detailed and broad ethnic groups where possible and when the data is available.

However, local authority data is only provided for the 6 broad ethnic groups. Information about the specific ethnic categories is excluded to preserve confidentiality and ensure individuals cannot be identified.

The 6 broad categories are as follows:

  • Asian/Asian British
  • Black/African/Caribbean/Black British
  • Chinese
  • Mixed/Multiple ethnic groups
  • White
  • Other

Summary

This data shows that:

  • in 2015/16, the highest permanent exclusion rate for Asian pupils was in Oldham (at 0.17%)

  • the highest permanent exclusion rate for Black pupils was in Dudley (at 0.60%)

  • for Chinese pupils, where data is available the permanent exclusion rate is 0.00% in every local authority – data is not provided for the some local authorities to preserve confidentiality where numbers are very small (fewer than 5 pupils)

  • the highest permanent exclusion rate for pupils from a Mixed ethnic background was 0.55%, in Solihull

  • the highest permanent exclusion rate for White pupils was 0.29%, which was the rate in North East Lincolnshire and Nottingham

  • the highest permanent exclusion rate for pupils from the Other ethnic group was 0.43%, which was the rate in Nottingham

Methodology

Methodology

Exclusions are reported by schools and collected through the school census every term.

Rates are calculated using the number of registered pupils as of the spring census (taken in January).

Schools use the agreed codes to record and monitor attendance and absence in a consistent way. You can see these codes in Annex C of the Department for Education’s Guide to exclusion statistics (PDF).

Both the number and rate of exclusions are included in published exclusions statistics. Rates of exclusion are a more appropriate measure for comparisons over time as they take into account changes in the total number of pupils across different academic years.

Suppression rules and disclosure control

Enrolment numbers at national and regional levels are rounded to the nearest 5.

Local authority totals across school types are also rounded to the nearest 5 to prevent any suppressed values being disclosed.

Enrolment numbers of less than 3 and rates based on fewer than 3 pupil enrolments have been suppressed. This is because, where the size of the ethnic group population is small enough that an individual’s identity could be revealed, information is suppressed to preserve confidentiality. The Code of Practice for Official Statistics requires DfE to take reasonable steps to ensure that their published or disseminated statistics protect confidentiality.

For more information about DfE’s disclosure control procedures for its statistical releases please see DfE’s statistical policy statement on confidentiality (PDF).

Rounding
